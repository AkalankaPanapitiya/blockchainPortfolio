Product Overview

The proposed study introduces a decentralized database system based on Blockchain Technology to address the limitations of Sri Lanka's existing identity verification and control system. The current system relies on centralized databases, which pose operational risks and security issues due to their vulnerability to attacks and manipulation of data. By transitioning to a decentralized database system, the trustworthiness and reliability of the system can be enhanced. A decentralized database system distributes control and eliminates the single point of failure, reducing the risk of unauthorized modifications and ensuring the integrity and security of data. Blockchain technology, known for its decentralized nature, offers a cryptographically protected, constantly expanding public ledger that enhances data management and transactions while avoiding the limitations of centralized databases. In a decentralized blockchain network, each member has an identical copy of the data, creating a trustless environment where data alteration requires consensus from the majority. Real-time, shared data views and optimized resource allocation further improve data reconciliation, integrity, and performance. The proposed decentralized database system aims to increase trust, security, and integrity in Sri Lanka's identity verification and control system. By leveraging Blockchain Technology, the system provides a more secure and trustworthy environment, alleviating concerns about data security and integrity for the government while ensuring genuine services for citizens.


What is the Problem?

The research problem to be addressed is the inadequate identity verification and control system in Sri Lanka, which relies on vulnerable hard copy documents and centralized digital systems. The prevalence of fraudulent identity documents poses a significant threat, eroding confidence in the authenticity of citizens' identities. The centralized digital identity systems have a single point of trust and failure, making them susceptible to attacks and data manipulation. To overcome these challenges, a blockchain-based citizen identity information management and verification system is proposed. Blockchain technology offers features such as confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information, providing a robust solution. However, existing blockchain systems, primarily based on Ethereum, suffer from energy inefficiency, scalability issues, and a lack of user control. The research problem focuses on developing a private permissioned blockchain system, specifically leveraging Hyperledger Fabric, to manage and verify Sri Lankan citizen identity information using a single identity. The system aims to empower citizens with control over the availability of their information while ensuring secure and reliable access for authorized non-government organizations. By incorporating essential features of citizen identity management and verification, this research project aims to address the existing limitations in the current system.


How we Addressed?

Throughout this research, we have undertaken several actions to overcome the problem stated in the research objectives. Our main objective has been to manage Sri Lankan citizen identity information and verification using a single identity, while granting citizens control over the availability of their information to non-government organizations through the utilization of blockchain technology. In order to increase the throughput of the identity management system, we selected a suitable blockchain development platform, specifically Hyperledger Fabric. This choice allowed us to optimize the system's performance and ensure efficient transaction processing, overcoming the limitations of traditional blockchain platforms.we developed distributed applications that interact with the blockchain ledger. These applications enable seamless management, verification, and control of citizen identity information. Through the use of decentralized applications (DApps), citizens, government organizations, non-government organizations, and foreign authorities can securely connect to the blockchain environment. This facilitates data verification and provides individuals with the ability to control the availability of their sensitive information.

Features of Blockchain

Decentralization

By removing the need for a central authority, blockchain enables peer-to-peer transactions, fostering trust and eliminating single points of failure.

Security

Utilizing cryptographic algorithms and distributed consensus, blockchain ensures data integrity and protects against unauthorized access and manipulation.

Transparency

Every transaction on the blockchain is recorded and visible to all participants, promoting accountability and trust among users.

Efficiency

Blockchain streamlines processes by automating transaction verification and reducing the need for intermediaries, resulting in faster and more cost-effective transactions.

Traceability

With every transaction being permanently recorded and timestamped, blockchain provides an immutable audit trail, allowing for tracing.
